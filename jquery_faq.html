National Parks FAQs

Q: What is a national park?
A: A national park is a protected area managed by a government agency to preserve natural, historical, or cultural resources.
Q: How many national parks are there in the United States?
A: As of 2021, there are 63 national parks in the United States.
Q: What is the oldest national park in the United States?
A: Yellowstone National Park, established in 1872, is the oldest national park in the United States.
Q: What is the most visited national park in the United States?
A: Great Smoky Mountains National Park, located in Tennessee and North Carolina, is the most visited national park in the United States.
Q: Can I bring my dog to a national park?
A: Dogs are allowed in some national parks, but they must be leashed at all times and are not allowed on trails or in most buildings.
Q: Do national parks have entrance fees?
A: Most national parks have entrance fees, which vary depending on the park and the type of entrance pass purchased.
Q: Can I camp in a national park?
A: Yes, camping is permitted in most national parks, but it is usually only allowed in designated campgrounds.
Q: Are there guided tours available in national parks?
A: Yes, many national parks offer guided tours led by park rangers or authorized tour guides.
Q: Can I fish in a national park?
A: Fishing is allowed in most national parks, but a fishing license is required and certain restrictions may apply.
Q: What should I do if I encounter a wild animal in a national park?
A: Visitors to national parks should always keep a safe distance from wildlife and not attempt to feed or approach them. If a wild animal appears threatening or aggressive, visitors should slowly back away and report the incident to park rangers.

National Parks Information

    Yellowstone National Park

    • Established in 1872
    • Covers 2.2 million acres
    • Home to over 300 geysers
    • Has a supervolcano beneath it

    Grand Canyon National Park

    • Established in 1919
    • Located in Arizona
    • Over 5 million visitors per year
    • Home to the Colorado River

    Yosemite National Park

    • Established in 1890
    • Located in California
    • Home to Half Dome and El Capitan
    • Has over 750 miles of hiking trails
